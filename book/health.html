Delayed Neurological Syndrome after Carbon Monoxide Poisoning.

There are two neurological syndromes observed with the same incidence: persistent neurological syndrome and delayed neurological syndrome.

In the latter, a period of lucidity is very typical between the manifestations of acute poisoning and a delayed involvement of the central

nervous system. After an apparent complete recovery, the following have been described: mental deterioration, deafness, visual impairment,

agnosia, temporal-spatial disorientation, and extrapyramidal symptoms. The manifestations can be either silent or dramatic. Among the most

common are: depression, memory loss, dementia, and parkinsonism. Brain autopsies reveal: petechiae, hemorrhages, and edema of the gray matter

with laminar necrosis, diffuse or focal cerebral atrophy with demyelination of the white matter.


Delayed Neurological Syndrome is observed in up to 40% of people who have been acutely or chronically poisoned with carbon monoxide.

The prognosis is highly variable. In general, 13% show severe neuropsychiatric disorders, 30% exhibit personality deterioration, and

40% suffer memory impairment. Advanced age and prolonged exposure are risk factors for the development of neurological sequelae.

Brain damage from chronic carbon monoxide poisoning is caused by the action of reactive oxygen species (superoxide anion, hydrogen peroxide,

and hydroxyl radical). In studies conducted, laboratory animals showed difficulty in learning and memory deficiency due to cell loss in the

cerebral cortex, cerebellum, and basal ganglia.


What Are the Benefits of Hyperbaric Medicine for Delayed Neurological Disorders?

Oxygen therapy improves late neurological disorders post-carbon monoxide poisoning in the following ways:

  • Relieves hypoxia.
  • Improves microcirculation.
  • Reduces cerebral edema through vasoconstrictive effects.
  • Preserves partially damaged tissue and prevents further progression of brain injury side effects.
  • Improves cerebral metabolism.
  • Reduces vascular damage by decreasing leukocyte adhesion to vessel walls.
  • Decreases oxidative stress by halting lipid peroxidation and glutathione depletion.
  • Protects against cellular apoptosis.
